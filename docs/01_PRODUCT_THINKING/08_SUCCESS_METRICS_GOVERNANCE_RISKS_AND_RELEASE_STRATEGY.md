# Product Requirements Document (PRD)

## Part VI - Success Metrics, Governance, Risks & Release Strategy

> *"A product is not complete when it is launched. It is complete only when it consistently creates value."*

---

# Why This Section Exists

Every successful engineering product requires more than a vision and an implementation plan.

It requires measurable outcomes, disciplined governance, continuous evaluation, and a structured evolution process.

This section defines how the AI Architect Knowledge Platform will be measured, reviewed, improved, and maintained throughout its lifecycle.

Its purpose is to ensure that the platform remains coherent as it grows.

---

# Product Success Definition

The platform succeeds when it consistently demonstrates engineering judgment, teaches valuable concepts, and reduces uncertainty for recruiters, engineering leaders, CTOs, collaborators, and future versions of myself.

Success is measured by trust rather than traffic.

Traffic is an output.

Trust is the objective.

---

# Success Metrics

## Objective 1 - Build Professional Trust

Measures

- Recruiters understand positioning within five minutes.
- Hiring managers explore multiple technical documents.
- CTOs continue beyond the homepage into architecture and philosophy.
- Visitors spend meaningful time reading long-form content.
- Repeat visitors increase over time.

Evidence

- Session depth.
- Returning visitor rate.
- Average document completion.
- Interview invitations.
- Professional referrals.

---

## Objective 2 - Demonstrate Engineering Judgment

Measures

- Architecture documents referenced during interviews.
- Case studies discussed in technical conversations.
- Engineering philosophy cited in discussions.
- Decision Framework adopted in future projects.

Evidence

- Interview feedback.
- Technical discussions.
- Community references.
- Repository stars and forks (secondary indicators).

## Objective 3 - Teach Engineering

Measures

- Documentation shared.

Evidence

- Returning readers.

Measures

# Governance Model

This platform follows documentation-first governance.

No significant implementation should occur without corresponding documentation.

Every major decision follows this sequence:

```text
Vision
  |
  v
Strategy
  |
  v
Requirement
  |
  v
Architecture
  |
  v
Implementation
  |
  v
Review
  |
  v
Documentation Update
  |
  v
Release
```

Documentation remains the canonical source of truth.

# Review Council

Every significant release is reviewed through seven perspectives.

## Chief Product Officer

Questions

- Does this support the product vision?
- Does it improve user value?
- Does it reduce unnecessary complexity?

---

## Chief Architect

Questions

- Is the architecture coherent?
- Are trade-offs documented?
- Can the design evolve?

---

## Engineering Manager

Questions

- Can the platform be maintained?
- Are standards followed?
- Is technical debt controlled?

---

## Technical Writer

Questions

- Is the documentation understandable?
- Does it teach?
- Is terminology consistent?

---

## Recruiter & Hiring Manager

Questions

- Does this strengthen credibility?
- Is the information easy to evaluate?
- Would this increase interview confidence?

---

## Brand Strategist

Questions

- Does this reinforce the intended professional identity?
- Is messaging consistent?
- Does every page feel like part of the same platform?

---

## Devil's Advocate

Questions

- Which assumptions are weak?
- Which claims require stronger evidence?
- What would a skeptical CTO challenge?
- Can every important statement be defended?

No major feature should bypass this review process.

# Risk Register

## Risk 1 - Scope Creep

Description

The platform grows into an unstructured collection of ideas.

Mitigation

Every new feature must trace back to Product Vision and Product Strategy.

---

## Risk 2 - Documentation Drift

Description

Implementation diverges from documentation.

Mitigation

Documentation-first workflow.

Mandatory updates before release.

---

## Risk 3 - Technology Chasing

Description

Frequent framework changes reduce stability.

Mitigation

Prioritize timeless engineering principles over short-lived technologies.

---

## Risk 4 - Personal Brand Inconsistency

Description

Different sections communicate different professional identities.

Mitigation

Brand document remains the canonical messaging source.

---

## Risk 5 - AI Hallucinated Contributions

Description

AI coding agents introduce undocumented assumptions.

Mitigation

Require AI agents to reference repository documents before implementation.

---

## Risk 6 - Knowledge Fragmentation

Description

Important lessons become duplicated across multiple documents.

Mitigation

Single Source of Truth (SSOT).

Cross-reference instead of duplication.

# Change Management

Every significant change should answer:

- Why is this changing?
- What evidence supports the change?
- Which documents are affected?
- Which AI prompts require updates?
- What migration work is necessary?

Changes should strengthen coherence.

Never weaken it.

---

# Definition of Product Success

Five years from now, success is not measured by:

- Number of pages.
- Number of projects.
- Number of technologies listed.

Success is measured by whether engineers, hiring managers, CTOs, and collaborators trust this platform as an authentic representation of engineering judgment.

The website is only the interface.

The real product is the accumulated knowledge behind it.

---

# Final Principle

The platform should remain an evolving engineering system rather than a finished portfolio.

Every new project should strengthen the existing architecture.

Every lesson should become reusable knowledge.

Every contribution should increase coherence.

Every decision should make future change easier.
